## Introduction

This test plan outlines the approach for testing the new eCommerce website. The objective is to **increase successful order completions by 15% within the first two months after launch by ensuring all critical user journeys are tested and defects are resolved before go-live** (SMART criteria).

---

## Entry Criteria (optional)

- All requirements and user stories are finalized and approved (optional)
- UI/UX wireframes provided (Optional)
- Test environment is set up and accessible (optional)
- Test data is prepared and available (optional)
- Test cases are written and reviewed (optional)
- All necessary resources (testers, tools) are assigned (optional)
- Development build is stable and deployed to the test environment (optional)

---

## In Scope

- Functional testing of product search, cart, checkout, and payment flows
- User registration and login
- Order history and account management
- Mobile and desktop compatibility
- Integration with payment gateways

---

## Types of Testing (optional)

- Functional Testing (optional)
- Regression Testing (optional)
- Integration Testing (optional)
- User Acceptance Testing (optional)
- Usability Testing (optional)
- Compatibility Testing (optional)
- Smoke Testing (optional)
- API Testing

---

## Out of Scope

- Performance/load testing
- Third-party marketing integrations
- Non-English language support

---

## Risks

- Delays in development may impact testing timelines
- Incomplete requirements may lead to missed test cases
- Limited access to production-like data

---

## Assumptions

- All requirements are finalized before testing begins
- Test environments will be stable and available
- Necessary test data will be provided

---

## Resource

- 2 QA engineers
- 1 test lead
- Support from development and product teams

---

## Timescales

- Test planning: 1 week
- Test execution: 3 weeks
- Bug fixing & retesting: 1 week
- Total: 5 weeks

---

## Environments + Tools

- Test environment mirroring production
- Selenium for automated testing
- Jira for defect tracking
- Chrome,

---

## Exit Criteria (optional)

- All planned test cases have been executed (optional)
- All critical and high-priority defects are fixed and verified (optional)
- Test summary report is completed and reviewed (optional)
- No major unresolved issues remain (optional)
- Stakeholders have signed off on test results (optional)
- Regression testing is completed with no critical failures (optional)
