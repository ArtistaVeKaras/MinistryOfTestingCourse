# Risk Escalation Procedure

A structured process to identify, assess, and address risks that exceed predefined thresholds or cannot be managed at the current level of authority. This ensures significant risks are promptly communicated to higher management or relevant stakeholders, enabling timely decision-making and mitigation.

---

## 1. Purpose

Define the process for escalating risks that require higher-level intervention and decision-making.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all departments and employees involved in risk management activities.

## 3. Definitions

- **Risk:** The potential for loss or harm.
- **Escalation:** The process of raising a risk to a higher level of authority for resolution.

## 4. Risk Identification

- Identify risks through regular monitoring, audits, and feedback.
- Document all risks in a Risk Register.

## 5. Risk Assessment

- Assess the severity and likelihood of each identified risk.
- Categorize risks using predefined criteria (e.g., low, medium, high).

## 6. Escalation Criteria

- Define clear thresholds for escalation (e.g., high impact or likelihood).
- Specify conditions requiring escalation (e.g., inability to mitigate at the current level, regulatory requirements).

## 7. Escalation Process

1. **Document** the risk details, including impact, likelihood, and current mitigation efforts.
2. **Notify** the immediate supervisor or risk manager.
3. **Escalate** to the next management level if the risk exceeds the supervisor's authority.
4. **Continue escalation** until the risk is addressed at the appropriate level.

## 8. Communication

- Use standardized forms or templates for risk escalation reports.
- Communicate escalated risks clearly and promptly to all relevant stakeholders.

## 9. Monitoring and Review

- Regularly review the status of escalated risks.
- Update the Risk Register and related documentation as needed.
- Track the effectiveness of mitigation actions.

## 10. Roles and Responsibilities

- **Employees:** Identify and report risks.
- **Supervisors:** Assess, document, and escalate risks as necessary.
- **Risk Managers:** Oversee the escalation process and ensure compliance with procedures.
- **Management:** Make decisions and allocate resources for risk mitigation.

## 11. Documentation

- Maintain comprehensive records of all escalated risks and actions taken.
- Ensure documentation is accessible for audits, reviews, and continuous improvement.

## 12. Training

- Provide regular training to employees on the risk escalation procedure.
- Ensure all staff understand their roles and responsibilities in the process.

---

**Tip:**  
Review and update the risk escalation procedure regularly to reflect organizational changes, lessons learned, and evolving best practices.
