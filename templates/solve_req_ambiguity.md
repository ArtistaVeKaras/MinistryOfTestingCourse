# Requirement Ambiguity Analysis & Clarification Template

Creating a template for analyzing every word in a requirement as a tester involves a systematic approach to ensure clarity, completeness, and testability. Below is a structured template that incorporates various techniques for requirement analysis. A systematic approach for testers to analyze, clarify, and refine requirements—ensuring they are clear, complete, and testable.

## 2. Contextual Analysis (The 5W1H)

| Aspect   | Guiding Questions                                                                 |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Who**  | Who is impacted or involved? (Stakeholders, users, systems)                       |
| **What** | What is the specific functionality or feature described?                          |
| **When** | Are there timing, frequency, or deadline constraints?                             |
| **Where**| Does this apply to specific environments, locations, or platforms?                |
| **Why**  | What is the business value or rationale?                                          |
| **How**  | How will this be implemented, used, or tested?                                    |

---

## 3. Requirement Classification

- **Functional:** Does it describe a system function or behavior?
- **Non-Functional:** Does it relate to performance, security, usability, etc.?
- **Special/Other:** Any unique, regulatory, or exceptional conditions?

---

## 4. Validation & Quality Checks

| Property      | Checkpoint                                                                |
|---------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Correctness**   | Is the requirement accurate and free from errors?                     |
| **Completeness**  | Are all necessary details and scenarios included?                     |
| **Feasibility**   | Is it realistic and achievable within constraints?                    |
| **Testability**   | Can it be tested? Are acceptance criteria clear?                      |
| **Ambiguity**     | Is it clear, specific, and unambiguous?                               |
| **Consistency**   | Does it align with other requirements and documentation?              |

## 7. Analysis Techniques

- **Gap Analysis:** Identify missing elements or mismatches with business needs.
- **Use Cases/User Stories:** Describe user interactions and flows.
- **Flowcharts/Diagrams:** Visualize processes, logic, or data flow.
- **Kano Model:** Assess impact on user satisfaction (basic, performance, delight).
- **Role-Activity Diagrams (RAD):** Map roles and activities for clarity.

---

## 8. Supporting Tools

- **Requirement Understanding Document (RUD):** Capture assumptions, details, and acceptance criteria.
- **Gantt Charts:** Visualize timelines for implementation and testing.
- **IDEF Models:** Model process functions and relationships for complex requirements.

---

**Tips:**

- Analyze every word—look for vague terms, missing actors, or implicit assumptions.
- Collaborate with stakeholders to resolve ambiguities early.
- Document all clarifications and decisions for traceability.

---

This template ensures each requirement is thoroughly analyzed, validated, and refined—reducing ambiguity and supporting effective testing and implementation.
