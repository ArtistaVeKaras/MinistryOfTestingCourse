# Activity: Map Key Skills to Essential Testing Tasks
**Time:** 45 minutes

## Purpose
This activity introduces you to common testing tasks and helps you recognize the skills and traits needed to complete them effectively. By completing the activity, you’ll better understand how different traits and skills play a role in real-world testing activities and how important it is to continually develop these skills. Soft skills, in particular, are often overlooked, yet they are essential for growth and success as a tester.

## Task Steps

### Scenario 1: Investigating a Bug Report
**Description:**  
You receive a bug report from a customer describing an issue with a login feature. The report lacks detailed steps to reproduce the problem. You need to figure out the exact steps to recreate the bug and provide a clear report to the development team.

**Skills and Attributes:**  
- Critical Thinking
- Problem Solving
- Communication

**Why:**  
Due to the lack of information in the report, the tester will need critical thinking and problem-solving skills to try to replicate what the user did and get the same outcome. If able to duplicate the scenario, the tester might find the solution. If not, good communication skills are needed to interact with the customer and the development team.

### Scenario 2: Collaborating on a New Feature
**Description:**  
Your team is building a new feature, and you’re attending a meeting with developers and the product manager. It’s your job to understand the requirements, ask relevant questions, and think about potential risks.

**Skills and Attributes:**  
- Collaboration
- Inquisitiveness
- Spotting Patterns

**Why:**  
Collaboration is essential. Inquisitiveness helps you fully understand requirements and identify risks.

### Scenario 3: Conducting Exploratory Testing
**Description:**  
You’ve been asked to perform exploratory testing on a new release to find issues not covered by pre-existing test cases.

**Skills and Attributes:**  
- Spotting Patterns
- Attention to Detail
- Critical Thinking

**Why:**  
Exploratory testing involves finding issues beyond expected behavior, requiring pattern spotting and critical thinking.

### Scenario 4: Prioritising Bugs for a Release
**Description:**  
There are multiple bugs, but not all can be fixed before the next release. You need to analyze impact and severity and collaborate with the team.

**Skills and Attributes:**  
- Time Management
- Adaptability
- Analytical Skills
- Communication

**Why:**  
Time management is crucial to focus on critical bugs. Communication is needed to explain decisions to the team.

## Mini Activities

### Goldilocks Heuristic: Contact Form (Phone Number)

- **Test Case 1:** Enter a phone number less than 11 characters long.
- **Test Case 2:** Enter a phone number exactly 11 characters long.
- **Test Case 3:** Enter a phone number more than 11 characters long.

### Heuristic Test Cases: Product Search

- Enter nothing in the search bar and click search.
- Enter a product name and click search.
- Enter multiple products and click search.

### Error Message Mnemonic

Trigger or recall an error message in software (e.g., login without username/password, overload a system). Record your findings in your portfolio.

### Oracles Research

Research oracles used in software testing and reflect on which oracles you use in everyday decisions. Update your portfolio with your findings.
