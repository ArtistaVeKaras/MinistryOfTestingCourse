# SMART Criteria for Goal Setting

The SMART framework is a proven method for setting effective and achievable goals. SMART stands for:

- **S – Specific:**  
  Goals should be clear, well-defined, and unambiguous. Specify exactly what you want to accomplish
  by addressing the "who, what, where, when, why, and how" of the objective.

- **M – Measurable:**  
  Goals must include measurable indicators to track progress and determine when the goal has been achieved. 
  Define concrete criteria, metrics, or benchmarks for success.

- **A – Achievable:**  
  Goals should be realistic and attainable, considering available resources, time, and capabilities. 
  While goals should be challenging, they must remain possible to accomplish.

- **R – Relevant:**  
  Goals need to align with broader objectives, values, and the overall mission. 
  Ensure the goal is meaningful and makes sense within the larger context.

- **T – Time-bound:**  
  Goals require a clear deadline or timeframe for completion. 
  Setting a target date creates urgency and helps maintain focus.

---

**Example of a SMART Goal:**  
_"Increase website traffic by 20% over the next three months by publishing weekly blog posts and promoting them on social media."_

This example is:

- **Specific:** Increase website traffic.
- **Measurable:** By 20%.
- **Achievable:** With regular content and promotion.
- **Relevant:** Supports business growth.
- **Time-bound:** Within three months.
