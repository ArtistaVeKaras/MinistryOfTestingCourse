# Main responsibilities as QA in Agile sprints

* Analysis of requirements, write and execute test cases to detect usability functional issues.
* Conducting test design, automate e2e tests, and exploratory testing.
* Bring a strategy-based approach to testing.
* Creating detailed reports and listing improvements.
* Participation in test planning meetings and providing feedback to the testing team, reviewing requirements specifications
and technical design documents to provide timely and meaningful feedback.
* Estimate, prioritize, plan, and coordinate testing activities, and work closely with product and development teams
to produce high-quality software.

## Quality Leadership

* Drive a shift-left quality mindset across the team; ensuring risks, assumptions, and edge cases are discussed early.
* Identify quality gaps in process, tooling, test strategy, or collaboration, and turn them into actionable improvements.
* Propose, lead, and deliver quality initiatives (e.g., risk-based testing, definition of test plans, automation improvements).
* Own and continuously improve the team’s quality strategy, including test approach, coverage, and best practices.

## Collaboration & Facilitation

* Work closely with PM and developers in refinement, design discussions, and Three Amigos sessions.
* Ensure quality is considered in every stage of the SDLC, from concept to release.
* Facilitate test planning for epics, features, and releases.
* Support developers in becoming stronger owners of their own testing activities.
* Foster a culture of psychological safety and constructive feedback when discussing quality concerns.

## Ownership & Accountability

* Take responsibility for ensuring releases meet the agreed quality bar.
* Drive documentation of quality learnings, risk registers, and regression strategies.
* Proactively surface quality issues with clear problem statements and suggested solutions.
* Track and communicate quality metrics to help guide decision-making (not vanity metrics).
