# Quality Assurance Process within the Software Development Life Cycle

## Planning & Requirements

I collaborate closely with product owners and developers through Three-Amigos workshops and refinement sessions.
This ensures requirements are clarified early, gaps are uncovered, and quality considerations are embedded from the start.

## Shift-Left Approach

I adopt a shift-left mindset, embedding testing activities as early as possible in the SDLC. This means introducing
automated unit and integration tests during development, validating requirements with BDD scenarios before coding,
and ensuring quality gates are part of every stage. By moving QA earlier, defects are detected sooner, reducing rework
and accelerating delivery.

## Test Design

I apply risk-based testing to prioritize coverage, and use BDD frameworks (e.g., Cucumber) to align business and
technical teams. Automation frameworks such as Selenium WebDriver, Playwright, and Cypress are leveraged to maximize
regression coverage and scalability.

## Execution

Automated test suites are integrated into CI/CD pipelines (e.g., Jenkins, GitHub Actions) to provide rapid feedback.
This ensures every code commit is validated against functional, regression, and smoke tests before deployment.

## Validation

I perform API testing and contract validation using tools like Postman and Bruno, alongside performance, security,
and accessibility checks. This ensures backend reliability and compliance with standards.

## Feedback & Reporting

I provide transparency through dashboards, metrics, and defect triage reports, enabling stakeholders to track
quality trends and make informed decisions.

## Continuous Improvement

Regression packs are continuously refined, manual cycles are replaced with automation, and lessons learned are
feedback into the process.This iterative improvement strengthens reliability and accelerates release velocity.
